The Journey of My Life

Childhood Years Teenage Years College Years

Childhood

Hi, I'm Luis Andrei Nario. I was born and raised in Tondo, Manila, a place where many people didn't want to go, hahaha. My childhood was simple and fun.

I loved playing outside with my friends, playing basketball, and running around. My family was always there to support me, and we had happy moments together. My father was a broker, and my mother stayed at home to take care of us.

Every morning, I helped with small chores before going to school. I liked history and P.E. because I loved playing basketball and learning new things about the world. After school, I played outside until it got dark.

On weekends, my friends and I played basketball until we got hungry. Sometimes, we told funny or scary stories to each other. My lolo made the best food, and we enjoyed eating together.

I also liked playing online games with my siblings. My parents always reminded me to study hard and reach for my dreams. I was so happy when I got my first ball, and I always brought it to the court.

I loved watching cartoons and playing Y8 with my siblings. Life was simple but full of love. During summer, we went to Zambales, where I learned how to swim. It was my first time at the beach, and I had so much fun.

When I was in grade 5, I joined the Boy Scouts at Magat Salamat Elementary School.

Teenage Years

My teenage years were full of challenges and new experiences. I started high school at Tondo High School. Entering high school was exciting but also scary because I had to make new friends and adjust to a new place.

At first, I was shy, but I became more confident as time passed. I joined school activities like group projects, class presentations, and sports events. One of my happiest moments was winning in the intramurals because it made me believe in myself.

I also started to explore my interests in music and technology. I enjoyed listening to music and tried to learn how to play the guitar. During weekends, I hung out with my friends at malls or fast-food restaurants. We laughed, talked, and had fun together.

But high school was not always easy because of schoolwork, peer pressure, and self-doubt. There were times when I didn't know what I wanted to do in the future. Some subjects were hard, but I always tried my best.

Even when I felt tired, I kept going because I wanted to do well in school. My teachers and parents always reminded me to work hard and believe in myself. I also learned the importance of true friendship.

Some friends stayed with me no matter what, while others slowly disappeared. High school taught me many things about life. It showed me that challenges make us stronger. Even though there were hard times, I am thankful for all the lessons I learned.

College Life

College is a new chapter in my life. I am studying at the University of the East, and it is both exciting and difficult. College means having more responsibilities and being more independent.

I had to adjust to a new schedule, manage my time, and balance my studies with my personal life. At first, I had a hard time with deadlines and exams, but I slowly learned how to stay organized.

In my first year, I tried out for the basketball intramurals, but sadly, I didn't pass. I felt disappointed, but I used it as motivation to improve. I realized that failure is part of life, and it helps us grow.

I didn't join any clubs or attend workshops because I wanted to focus on my studies. Even though college is hard, I met good friends who helped me through tough times. We studied together and supported each other.

However, this January, I went through a really painful experience. My girlfriend cheated on me, and it made me feel very sad and lost. I fell into depression, and I couldn't focus on my studies. Because of this, I failed some of my subjects in the second semester.

It was one of the hardest moments of my life, but it taught me the importance of mental health. Now, I am okay, and I'm focusing on subjects that I failed. Some subjects are hard, but I try my best to understand them.

College is helping me grow as a person. Even though there are many challenges, I know I will keep moving forward.
